# Brand Strategy

## Brand Architecture

| Layer | Name | Role |
|-------|------|------|
| **Company** | **Ora** | The intelligence platform. Holds the long-term vision -- social infrastructure, the AI layer powering human connection at scale. Named after the oracle intelligence at the core of everything built. |
| **Product** | **Aura** | The consumer app. The first product Ora builds. Warm, accessible, immediately understood. What users download, talk about, and experience. |
| **AI entity** | **Ora** | The intelligence inside Aura. The oracle that understands your aura and finds your people. What users interact with directly. "Ask Ora." "Ora found your group." |

**The relationship:** Ora sees your aura. The product is named after the thing the AI perceives and creates -- the energy of human connection. Aura is what you experience. Ora is what makes it possible.

**Why two names:**
- Aura greets people warmly. It does emotional work the moment someone reads it -- everyone knows what an aura is, and it maps directly onto the product's vibe mechanic.
- Ora carries the intelligence and mystery. It has the slightly uncanny, 2050-aesthetic quality that lives inside the product, not on the landing page.
- The company is named after its intelligence (Ora), not its first product (Aura) -- which holds the platform vision as new products are built.

## Positioning

This product occupies a space that does not currently exist in a clear form: **a friend group formation engine**.

It is not a dating app. It is not a networking app. It is not a meetup platform. It is the thing you use when you have moved to a new city, started a new chapter, or just want a real social circle -- and you want it without the randomness, the awkwardness, or the effort of organizing it yourself.

The clearest one-line positioning:

> **We find your people, then keep you together.**

Everything before "then" is the discovery phase. Everything after is the retention play. Both halves need to be felt in the brand.

---

## The Emotional Territory

The product lives in a very specific emotional moment:

**The gap between "I'm socially adrift" and "I found my crew."**

That gap is real for a huge number of people -- new-to-city professionals, expats, people post-university, people who outgrew their existing social circle. The pain is not loneliness in the extreme sense. It is more subtle: social life feels effortful, random, and low-yield. You go to things, meet people, and nothing sticks.

The brand needs to speak to this without making users feel pathetic for being there. This is not a product for people who can't make friends. It is a product for people who are good at life but don't have the right context right now.

**Emotional promise:** You will stop feeling like your social life is up to chance.

---

## Brand Personality

If this product were a person:

- Warm, but not gushing. It cares about you without being over the top about it.
- Smart, but wears it lightly. You feel the intelligence in the results, not in how it talks to you.
- Confident without being arrogant. It makes a promise and keeps it, without overselling.
- Has a quiet sense of humor. Life is social, this should feel alive -- not sterile or corporate.
- Gets straight to the point. Low friction is a core product value; the brand voice should reflect that too.

**Archetype:** The brilliant friend who somehow always knows exactly who you should meet and where you should go. Not a matchmaker. Not a concierge. A friend with great instincts and zero agenda.

---

## What It Must NOT Feel Like

These are hard guardrails. If a name, color, copy line, or design choice evokes any of these, it is wrong:

- **A dating app.** No swipe mechanics, no profile-heavy vibes, no "find love" language adjacent territory.
- **A networking app.** No LinkedIn energy. No "professional connections" or "grow your network" framing. This is about genuine human friendship.
- **A therapy or self-help product.** No heavy emotional language, no "healing your social anxiety" territory. Light, confident, forward-moving.
- **A corporate event platform.** Meetup.com is functional and cold. This should feel alive and personal.
- **A financial product.** No fintech energy. No dashboard-heavy, metrics-driven, transactional feel. This is warm and human, not clinical and optimized.
- **Generic tech.** No cold blues, no "AI-powered" badge-wearing, no tech-bro minimalism. The AI is invisible. The humanity is visible.

## Brand Values

1. **Genuine over performative.** Real friendships over engineered connections.
2. **Effortless over complicated.** We handle the complexity so users never feel it.
3. **Patient over instant.** Friend groups take a few activities to form. We respect that process instead of rushing it.
4. **Confident over cautious.** We make a recommendation and stand behind it. No "you might like" hedging.

---

## Target Audience Emotional Profile

Not demographic -- emotional:

- Capable, independent person who handles most of life well
- Recently changed context (new city, new job, post-uni, post-relationship, post-pandemic reset)
- Socially comfortable but socially under-resourced right now
- Tired of low-yield social effort (meetups that lead nowhere, apps that feel hollow)
- Would not describe themselves as "lonely" but would privately admit their social life is not where they want it
- Would pay to skip 6 months of trial and error if the outcome was a real friend group

---

## Tone of Voice

**Concise.** Never more words than needed.

**Direct.** States the promise clearly, does not hedge.

**Warm but grounded.** Feels human without being saccharine.

**Subtly confident.** Implies "we know what we are doing" without saying it.

**Examples of what the voice sounds like:**

- "Show up. We handled the rest."
- "You don't need to plan anything. Just be there."
- "Your people are already in the pool. We just need to find the right combination."
- "5 Plans. Your people." (the journey is shown, not explained)

**Examples of what the voice does NOT sound like:**

- "Connect with amazing people who share your passions!" (too generic/dating-app)
- "Our AI-powered matching algorithm ensures..." (too technical/corporate)
- "Find your tribe." (overused, slightly cult-y)
- "You deserve meaningful connections." (therapy-adjacent, preachy)

## Product Terminology

Consistent language matters for brand coherence. These are the canonical terms:

| Use | Not |
|-----|-----|
| **Aura** | the app, the platform, the product |
| **Ora** | the AI, the algorithm, the system |
| **Plan** / **Plans** | activity, event, meetup, session |
| **Group** | match group, cohort, cluster |
| **Your people** | matches, connections, network |
| **Find your people** | meet new people, make friends, connect |
| **Vibe** | rating, score, feedback, compatibility |
| **Ask Ora** | talk to the AI, use the chatbot |

**On the 5-Plan journey:**
The process (5 Plans to find your group) is communicated visually on onboarding -- a simple visual progression sets the expectation upfront. Copy does not need to explain or justify the process. Users understand it before they start; the brand voice never has to reassure them mid-journey.

**On Ora as the AI entity:**
Ora is not described as an AI assistant or chatbot. It is referred to as an entity with instincts. "Ora found your people." "Ora is learning who you click with." "Trust Ora." Never "our AI" or "the algorithm."
