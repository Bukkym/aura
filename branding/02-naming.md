## The Story Behind the Names

**Aura** is the energy, presence, and feeling that surrounds a person -- what others perceive before they can explain why. It maps directly onto the product's core mechanic: vibe, the invisible compatibility between people that Ora reads and matches. The product is named after the thing it creates and perceives.

**Ora** comes from two roots simultaneously:
- **Oracle** -- an intelligence that knows things before you do. The AI that sees human compatibility at a level you cannot consciously articulate.
- **Aura** -- Ora is embedded in the word Aura itself. The AI is named after what it understands.

**The relationship:** Ora sees your aura. Four words. The entire product concept.

---

## Why This Structure Works

**Aura as the product name:**
- Warm and immediately understood -- no explanation needed
- Everyone has a felt sense of what an aura is
- "Welcome to Aura" works perfectly as a landing page opener
- "Find your Aura" as a tagline is intuitive
- Sounds premium, human, and distinctive in the App Store
- Does not say AI, dating, networking, or social -- it owns a fresh category

**Ora as the AI entity:**
- Has the mysterious, slightly uncanny quality of a 2050-aesthetic AI
- Carries the oracle intelligence -- it knows who you belong with
- Natural in voice-first interaction: "Ask Ora", "Ora found your group"
- Referred to as an entity with instincts, never as a chatbot or algorithm
- Gives the AI a personality without making the product feel cold

**Ora as the company:**
- A platform company named after its intelligence, not its first product
- Holds the long-term vision: social infrastructure, the AI layer for human connection
- As new products are built beyond Aura, Ora is the entity that powers them
- Fundable as a platform story: "Ora builds Aura. Aura is the first product."

## Naming Journey (Summary)

Explored 50+ names across multiple directions:

- **Warm/human:** Found, Kindred, Kith, Kin, Often, Hearth, Set, Circle, Belong
- **AI/futuristic:** Ora, Aether, Arc, Anima, Known, Bureau, Numen, Mana, Orenda
- **Combination:** OraKin, OraFolk, OraVibe, OraKnown, Aurai
- **Aura synonyms:** Numen, Mana, Verve, Brio, Orenda, Élan, Ardor

Key filters that eliminated candidates:
- Trademark conflicts (Venn, Grove, Gather, Among, Aura dating app, Crew, Band)
- Pronunciation ambiguity (Aurai, Ruah, Élan)
- Category limitation (Kith/Kin limit platform expansion)
- Romantic tone (Found)
- Generic tech feel (Link, Connect, Match)

**The breakthrough:** treating Aura as the product and Ora as the AI entity solved the tension between "warm and immediately understood" (Aura) and "mysterious and AI-forward" (Ora). Both names survive. Both do different jobs.

**The honest verdict:**

- **Class 045:** One direct conflict — AURA DATING (dating/matchmaking, PENDING, individual owner). The key legal question is whether "friendship group formation" is sufficiently distinct from "dating and matchmaking" under Class 045. These are related but meaningfully different services. An attorney can assess likelihood of confusion and whether a well-differentiated description survives examination.
- **Class 042:** Heavily contested, especially by Salesforce. A social app's software would need to be described with precision (social matching, group formation) to avoid overlap with Salesforce's "scientific and technological services" framing. The key mitigant: Salesforce's Class 042 registration covers enterprise dev tools, not consumer social apps — but Salesforce can and does challenge marks they perceive as adjacent.
- **Bottom line:** The name is registrable with the right attorney and the right description of services. It is not a clean clear — it requires a formal opinion. Do not file or fundraise under AURA before commissioning a trademark clearance opinion from a US trademark attorney. Cost: $300–500.

**Ora — lower risk:**
- Registered ORA trademark found in clinical trial consulting only -- different class entirely
- No registered conflicts found in Class 42 (social software) or Class 45 (social services) via web research
- Still requires formal clearance before filing

**Domain landscape (Aura):**
Aura.com is taken (identity protection). All obvious combinations (tryaura, getaura, joinaura, etc.) require checking. The .ai TLD is worth prioritising given the product vision.

**Domain landscape (Ora):**
Extremely crowded. Nearly all obvious combinations are registered. ora.io expires May 2026 (backorder opportunity). heyora.com is listed for sale. joinora.io returned no WHOIS data (check on registrar directly).

**Recommended domain strategy:**
Secure a clean domain for Aura for the landing page. The company domain (Ora) can follow once trademark clearance is confirmed. Priority order: aura.ai > tryaura.com > joinaura.com > aura.app.
